FAQ

Common questions about COVID-19

Which provider do I choose?

If you have insurance it is recommended to go to a private testing center as the fees for the test will most likely be covered and the testing process will be quicker and much easier. If you do not have insurance a government testing center is recommended as the tests are free, but your results may take longer and it may be difficult to get your test done.

What happens if I am sent back from my testing site?

You can show proof of your scheduled appointment and that you passed the pretest or met all requirements to get tested. Make sure you have checked all the requirements to get tested at your site.

What do I do if I test positive?

If you do test positive for COVID-19, you must self quarantine from everyone including your family. Make sure you wash your hands and wear a face mask.

What type of test do I take?

If you want to get quick results take a Rapid Antigen Test, but these are less accurate than PCR tests. Make sure your insurance covers the Rapid Antigen Test if you do want to take it. It is recommended to take the PCR test as the results are much more accurate and if you do test negative they want to make sure the patient is actually negative.

What are some symptoms or signs that I have the coronavirus?

If you have a fever, cough, shortness of breath, muscle aches, headache, new loss of taste or smell, sore throat, congestion or runny nose, and other common cold symptoms.
